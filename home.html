How to Play NBA TopShot?

Is NBA TopShot an NFT?

NFTs (non-fungible tokens) are certificates of ownership of digital objects - from works of art to video game items. Recently, NFTs have moved from art and fashion to other fields, in this case, the world of sports.

NBA TopShot is a new way to experience basketball as a fan. TopShot is a blockchain-based platform built on Flow.

The platform creates and sells a type of NFT. NFTs use blockchain technology to create unique, digital assets - usually collectibles - that can be verified as genuine and one-of-a-kind.

It allows basketball fans to buy, sell, and trade officially-licensed digitally captured highlights as NFTs called Moments. By collecting Moments, fans can get rewarded along with having the ultimate fan bragging rights. These licensed digital collectables are similar to virtual basketball cards.

What is a Moment?

Moments are digitally captured highlights from the past and present. Moments can be aquired through pack drops, challenges, and the Marketplace.

Think of each one as a digital version of a traditional sports card, only with a few advantages. First, there’s essentially no chance of buying a counterfeit moment: each one is “minted” with a unique serial number on the Flow blockchain, and you can access a complete transaction history for your specific version.

What is the Marketplace?

The Marketplace is where NBA TopShot Moments are bought and sold. It is a digital platform for NBA fans to buy and trade video clips (moments) of games. The moments are sold as NFTs and are approved by the NBA.

Some of them have been purchased and traded for tens, and hundreds of thousands of dollars. A mmoment of Lebron James offering a dunk in memory of Kobe Byrant was sold for nearly $387,000.

While these clips can be viewed online, what buyers are interested in is mostly ownership. Anyone can view the video online, but only the purchaser of the NFT can boast ownership.
